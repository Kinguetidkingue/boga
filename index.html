Learn HTML

HTML is a markup language and stands for HyperText Markup. HTML is the main technology to use in order to create web pages. HTML is the backbone of the Internet and is widely used for web development.

Learn CSS

CSS is a technological tool that stands for Cascading Style Sheet. CSS is used to style web pages by adding colors, fonts and layouts. CSS is essential for creating elegant and responsive web pages. CSS is the second sofware to be invented.

Learn Javascript

Javascript is a versatile programming language for web development. It is known for its rich ecosystem of libraries and frameworks and is a popular choice for building interactive web applications.

Learn Bootstrap

Bootstrap is a framework that gathers three technologies: HTML for the structure, CSS for the styling and JS for interactivity. With Bootstrap you can fastly build web applications.

From coast to coast, Canada is a pluralistic country with many diversities

City of Montreal

Montreal is the largest city in Canada's province of Quebec and the largest french-speaking city in the Americas. It is known for its culture, arts, festivals, and vibrant nightlife. Montreal has many parks and green spaces. It is home to "LE CANADIEN DE MONTREAL".

City of Ottawa

Ottawa is the federal capital of Canada - located in the province of Ontario. Being the political center of Canada, Ottawa is home to many government buildings and embassies. Ottawa is known for its historic landmarks and tulip festival. It is home to OTTAWA Senators.

City of Toronto

Toronto is the largest city in Canada and the capital of the province of Ontario. It is known for its diverse population, vibrant arts and culture scene, and iconic landmarks such as the CN Tower. Toronto is a financial and business hub and home to TORONTO Maple Leafs.

City of Vancouver

Vancouver is a coastal city located in the province of British Columbia, Western Canada. It is known for its stunning natural beauty, with mountains like the Rockies, forests, and the Pacific Ocean surrounding the city. Vancouver is home to the NHL team Vancouver Canucks.

Each province not only has its own capital-city but also its typical specificity

The City of Fredericton

Fredericton is the capital of Canada's province of New Brunswick. It is home to provincial institutions headquarters. Fredericton is crossed by the Saint-John river flowing to the Atlantic Ocean. Wintertime is really tougher given the closer and geographical postion of the Maritimes provinces to the Atlantic.

The City of Calgary

Calgary is the main city of the province of Alberta-CANADA. Calgary is a vibrant city with an economy mainly based on the oil and gas industry and tourism. Calgary is home to natural beauties and a gateway to the Rockies Mountains. It is also home to the NHL team, the Flames and annually hosts the Stampede.

The City of Winnipeg

Winnipeg is the capital of Canada's province of Manitoba located in the Prairies. As many other cities in Manitoba, it is home to Metis people whose founder has been Louis Riel. The Jets of Winnipeg, a NHL team is based in Winnipeg. Summer is an ideal time for outdoors including festivals or other activities

The City of Halifax

Halifax is the capital and main city of Canada's province of Nova Scotia. It's a major economic center in Atlantic Canada. It has a rich history and is known for its maritime culture. As a coastal city, Halifax has a strong connection to the sea and is home to Canada Marines base. The city has many historic sites and museums.

The City of St John's

St John's is the main city of Canada's province of Newfoundland. Given its geographical and closer location to water, St John's has a rich culture to the sea and especially in the fishing industry that is the main source of economical activities. It is isolated to mainland Canada because Newfounland is a Maritimes Island.

The City of Edmondton

Edmonton is the provincial capital of Canada's Alberta and is the northernmost city often called the GAteway to the North. Edmonton is known for its festivals, arts and culture scene, and beautiful river valley parks. It is home to the NHL team "The Oilers". The city has a strong economy based on oil and gas industry.

The City of Charlottetown

Charlottetown is the capital and largest city of Canada's Prince Edwards Island. It's famously known as the birth place of the Confederation of Canada because the Charlottetown Conference, a gathering between Canadian and Maritimes statemen to discuss the Maritimes union in 1864 happened there.

The City of Iqualit

Iqualit is the main city of the territory of Nanvut located in the geographical region of Northern Canada. Because of this position Iqualit is close to the artic pole and has a cold weather meaiig long winter and short summers. Boreal aurors are part of the beauties of the area.

The City of Yellowknife

Yellowknife is one of the northernmost cities of Canadian North West territories. Geography makes it it too close to the artic pole. While boreal aurors are astoningly epic and make a well-being emotion given the marvels of beauties of mother nature, winter is long and tougher to live.
